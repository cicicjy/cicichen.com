02 / Field notes

Judgments I return to.

Four working beliefs drawn from product, category, and consumer-facing decisions.

01 / Product beliefBegin →

Good products help people begin.

A good product is not a bundle of features. It understands the user’s body, emotions, and relationships—and turns unspoken hesitation into enough safety to take action.

02 / Commercial judgmentValue →

Value for money is not low price.

Value is designed when users understand why something is worth buying, channels have a reason to sell it, and the brand can still build long-term equity.

03 / Consumer insightState →

Design for the state after the sip.

Beverage innovation begins with the physical and emotional state a person wants next: refreshed, awake, lighter, restored, or cared for.

04 / Product expressionOrder →

Packaging is an information order.

Packaging is not decoration. It decides what a person sees first, what they understand, what they trust, and what they remember before a choice is made.
